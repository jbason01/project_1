The Pontiac Firebird (1967-2002)

The Pontiac Firebird was a front-engine muscle car produced by Pontiac between the years 1967 and 2002. It was a competitor to the Ford Mustang, and was built on the same chassis as the Chevrolet Camaro.

The first two generations of the Pontiac Firebird span from 1967–1969 and 1970–1981. The second generation ended in 1981, with the third generation kicking off in 1982. It ran until 1997, when the fourth gen was introduced, and the following year, in 1998 the new LS1 engine was added.
